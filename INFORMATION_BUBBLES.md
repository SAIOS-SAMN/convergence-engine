## The Concept

On the current web, sharing information means exposing
flat posts with no context, no domain classification,
and no containment. Everyone sees everything the same way.

The bubble model inverts this. Instead of exposing your
entire presence, you detach a self-contained information
orb from your personal solar system and hand it to
someone. The bubble carries:

- Its own domain classification (verified, working, creative)
- Its own depth (summary at surface, detail on zoom)
- Its own verification level (proven, cited, speculative)
- Its own context (where it sits in the larger architecture)

The recipient holds the bubble. They see the surface. If
they want more, they zoom in. If they don't, they move on.

They never see your solar system. They never see your
core. They never see your archive. They receive ONE piece
of information with its own built-in context.
