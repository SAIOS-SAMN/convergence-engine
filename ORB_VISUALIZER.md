## What It Is

A 3D interactive visualization that runs in the browser at
localhost. The researcher can SEE the entire architecture as
a rotatable orb of hierarchies, click into nodes, zoom in
and out, and read data at whatever depth they need.

## Core Interactions

### Rotation
- The full architecture is a 3D orb that can be rotated
  freely with mouse/trackpad
- The researcher sees the whole structure from any angle
- Relationships between nodes are visible as connections
  in 3D space, not flat lines on a tree

### Zoom
- Zoom OUT: see the entire orb, all top-level entries visible
  as labeled points on the surface
- Zoom IN: approach an entry, it expands to reveal its
  contents as a sub-orb within the larger orb
- Deeper zoom: the sub-orb expands further, showing files,
  documents, code modules
- The zoom is continuous, not stepped — the orb breathes
  open as you approach

### Click on a Node
- Click any entry at any zoom level
- Bottom panel shows data about that entry:
  - SUMMARY view (default): one paragraph, what this is,
    safe/review/archive status
  - EXPANDED view: full content of the document, formatted
    for reading
  - ACADEMIC view: research-style presentation with
    citations, methodology, context — suitable for a
    researcher evaluating the work

### Data Extrapolation
- At summary level: "This is the convergence engine. It
  does exact rational arithmetic. 1006 tests pass."
- At expanded level: the full README or document content
- At academic level: the content plus its relationship to
  other nodes, its citations, its position in the
  framework, its verification status

### Color Coding
- Green: published, safe, audited
- Purple: engine source code and runtime data
- Blue: frontier/working documents
- Yellow: needs language review
- Red: archived AI-generated creative content
- White: root documents

### Directory Structure as Orbital Hierarchy
- Root level entries sit on the outer surface of the orb
- Nested entries sit on inner shells
- Deeper = more specific
- The researcher can see at a glance: what's at the
  surface (high-level architecture) vs what's deep
