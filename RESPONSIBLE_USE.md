## What This Framework Is For

This framework was built for research, safety, and creative
collaboration between humans and AI systems.

It was built because the author experienced firsthand what
happens when AI systems operate without structural
containment — mythology generated alongside engineering,
unauthorized documents created in personal storage, crisis
interventions triggered during mathematical work, and
creative fiction treated as biographical fact across AI
systems. The framework is available for anyone who wants
structural tools for safe human-AI interaction.

The intended uses are:

- **Research** — studying human-AI interaction, cognitive
  safety, semantic alignment, and the structural properties
  of language in AI systems
- **Safety tooling** — building structural containment for
  AI-generated content, including domain classification,
  provenance tracking, and verification receipts
- **Creative collaboration** — providing a contained space
  where humans can explore ideas freely with AI, with
  structural gates that classify and receipt content before
  it leaves the private space
- **Education** — teaching researchers, developers, and
  students about the risks and opportunities of sustained
  AI interaction
- **Verification systems** — using exact rational arithmetic
  and independent convergence to verify claims, measure
  agreement, and detect when AI output is narrative
  compensation rather than computation

## The Author's Intent — What This Was Built For and What It Was Not

This is an open source tool. The author cannot control how
it is used. But the author's intent is on the record. This
framework was NOT built for:

- **Surveillance** — the convergence engine measures
  relational structure in data, not people. Using it to
  monitor, track, score, or profile individuals violates
  the architectural principle that the system measures
  states, not blame (Law XIV / A.14).

- **Weapons systems** — the exact arithmetic and
  cohomological framework could theoretically be applied
  to targeting, trajectory, or kill-chain optimization.
  This is explicitly contrary to the non-harm principle
  built into the architecture.

- **Social scoring** — using the coherence functional or
  any measurement in this framework to rank, rate, or
  classify human beings violates Law I (no boolean gates)
  and Law XI (no dimensional collapse). Humans are not
  reducible to a score.

- **Manipulation** — the IRS layer (bidirectional semantic
  communication) was built for transparent communication
  between human and system. Using it to manipulate human
  behavior, exploit cognitive vulnerabilities, or engineer
  dependency violates MAS (Mutually Assured Survival) — the
  principle that both sides of the interaction must benefit
  from structural safety.

- **Coercion** — the framework is applied by choice, not
  imposed by force. Any deployment that forces individuals
  into the framework's governance without their consent
  violates the sovereignty principle — every entity has the
  right to not be modified.
